# Groups vs Sectors (Category Groups) - User Guide

## Overview

After the refactoring, **Sectors are now Category Groups** - a special type of Group. Here's how users will use them:

---

## ðŸŽ¯ **Category Groups (Formerly Sectors)**

### What They Are
- **Pre-defined technology categories** created by admins
- **Public groups** that anyone can join
- **Competition-focused** - compete with others in your field
- Examples: Web Development, AI/ML, Mobile Development, etc.

### When Users Use Category Groups

1. **Finding Your Niche**
   - Browse categories at `/sectors`
   - See which technology field interests you
   - Join categories relevant to your learning path

2. **Competing in Your Field**
   - Join "Web Development" if you're learning React, Node.js, etc.
   - Join "AI/ML" if you're learning Python, TensorFlow, etc.
   - Compete on category-specific leaderboards

3. **Tracking Progress**
   - See your rank within a specific category
   - Compare your progress with others in the same field
   - Get motivated by seeing top performers in your category

4. **Organizing Courses**
   - When creating a course, you can assign it to a category group
   - Helps organize learning by technology stack
   - Makes it easier to find relevant courses

### Example Use Cases

**Student Learning Web Development:**
- Joins "Web Development" category
- Creates courses in that category
- Competes on the Web Development leaderboard
- Sees how they rank among other web developers

**Professional Learning AI:**
- Joins "AI/ML" category
- Shares AI projects in that category
- Tracks progress against other AI learners
- Builds reputation in the AI community

## ðŸ‘¥ **Custom Groups**

### What They Are
- **User-created groups** for teams, communities, or organizations
- **Private or Public** - creator controls access
- **Social/Collaborative** - work together with friends, classmates, or colleagues
- Examples: "My College CS Group", "Hackathon Team 2024", "Study Group"

### When Users Use Custom Groups

1. **Creating Study Groups**
   - Create a private group for your college class
   - Invite classmates to join
   - Share courses and projects within the group
   - Compete only within your group

2. **Team Projects**
   - Create a group for your hackathon team
   - Collaborate on projects
   - Track team progress
   - Private group for team-only competition

3. **Organizations**
   - Companies can create groups for employees
   - Universities can create groups for students
   - Coding bootcamps can create groups for cohorts

4. **Friend Groups**
   - Create a group with friends learning together
   - Share resources and motivate each other
   - Friendly competition within the group

### Example Use Cases

**College Class:**
- Professor creates "CS101 Fall 2024" group
- Students join the group
- All assignments and projects are in this group
- Students compete only with classmates

**Hackathon Team:**
- Team leader creates "Hackathon2024-TeamAlpha" group
- Team members join
- All hackathon projects are in this group
- Team tracks progress together
